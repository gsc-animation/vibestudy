# Educational Framework: VibeStudy Methodology

## 1. Introduction
VibeStudy is built upon a synthesis of three powerful educational frameworks: **CLIL** (Content and Language Integrated Learning), **CPA** (Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract), and **Agile** (Project Management for Education). This document details how these theories translate into tangible software features for Grade 3 students.

## 2. CLIL: Integrating Science & English
For ESL students, learning Science *in* English requires a dual focus on content and language.

### 2.1. The 4Cs Framework in VibeStudy
*   **Content:** The core scientific concepts (e.g., Magnetism, Life Cycles).
*   **Communication:** The language needed to describe the content.
    *   *Feature:* **Sentence Frames**. Users don't just click answers; they build sentences like *"The magnet pulled the clip because..."*.
*   **Cognition:** Developing thinking skills (Bloom's Taxonomy).
    *   *Feature:* **Prediction Engine**. Before an experiment, students must predict the outcome (Hypothesizing/Evaluating).
*   **Culture:** Connecting learning to the real world.
    *   *Feature:* **"Did You Know?" Cards**. Real-world applications (e.g., Maglev trains) linked to lessons.

### 2.2. Vocabulary Tiers & Support
*   **Tier 1 (Basic):** Everyday words (*push, hot*).
*   **Tier 2 (Academic):** Cross-curricular words (*observe, compare*).
*   **Tier 3 (Subject-Specific):** Technical words (*friction, photosynthesis*).
*   **Software Strategy:** 
    *   **Contextual Glossary:** Tier 3 words are hyperlinked within lessons.
    *   **Sandwich Method:** Explain a concept in simple English -> Introduce the Tier 3 term -> Recap in simple English.

## 3. From CPA to VRA (Virtual-Representational-Abstract)
The Singapore Math CPA model is adapted for digital science learning as **VRA**.

### 3.1. Virtual (The "Concrete" Phase)
*   **Concept:** Replacing physical manipulatives with interactive digital simulations that provide immediate visual/auditory feedback.
*   **Implementation:** 
    *   *Physics:* A "Virtual Lab" where students can change the surface material (sand, ice) and see a car speed up or slow down.
    *   *Feedback:* Visual recoil when magnets repel; sound effects when they snap together.

### 3.2. Representational (The "Pictorial" Phase)
*   **Concept:** Visualizing the invisible forces or data trends.
*   **Implementation:** 
    *   *Overlay Mode:* After the car runs, an overlay allows students to draw arrows (vectors) representing Friction and Gravity.
    *   *Interactive Charts:* Students drag bars to represent the distance traveled on different surfaces.

### 3.3. Abstract (The Symbolic Phase)
*   **Concept:** Using symbols and academic language to define the rule.
*   **Implementation:** 
    *   *Cloze Exercises:* "Friction is a force that [slows down] moving objects."
    *   *Formula Building:* Dragging variables to form simple relationships (Speed = Distance / Time - simplified).

## 4. Agile for Kids (eduScrum)
Teaching executive function and self-regulation through managed workflows.

### 4.1. The Weekly Sprint
*   **Planning:** Students and parents sit down (Sunday night) to move tasks from "Backlog" to "My Mission" (Weekly To-Do).
*   **Autonomy:** Students choose *when* to do which subject during the week, fostering ownership.

### 4.2. Kanban Mechanics
*   **Limit Work in Progress (WIP):** The "Doing" column only allows **one** task at a time. This prevents multitasking and cognitive overload.
*   **Visual Progress:** Moving a task to "Done" triggers a dopamine-rewarding animation.

### 4.3. Retrospective (Metacognition) & AI Insights
*   **The Student's Voice:** At the end of the week, a "Retro Wizard" asks:
    1.  *What went well?* (Celebrate)
    2.  *Where did I get stuck?* (Identify Blockers)
    3.  *Plan for next week.* (Adapt)
*   **The AI Scrum Master:**
    *   Parents receive an AI-generated synthesis of these reflections combined with performance data.
    *   *Example:* "Your child felt confident in 'Magnets' but marked 'Friction' as a blocker. The test data confirms this with a 60% score on Friction questions. **Action:** Review the 'Surface Area' simulation."
    *   This bridges the gap between raw data and pedagogical action.

| **VRA (Virtual)** | Physics Engine Simulations | Makes abstract forces (friction, magnetism) visible and tangible. |
